# QAP End User Guide

## Signing In

1. Open the QAP web application.
2. Select **Sign In**.
3. Enter your email or username and password.
4. After successful login, the system sends you to the dashboard for your role.

If you cannot log in, check that your email/username is correct. Use password reset if your account supports email reset.

## Student Guide

### Dashboard

The student dashboard shows:

- Total submissions.
- Resolved submissions.
- Reports currently in review.
- Pending reports.
- Recent submissions.

### Submit a Report

1. Open **New Submission**.
2. Choose the submission type, such as complaint or suggestion.
3. Enter the report title and description.
4. Select faculty, department, category, urgency, and related details.
5. Choose privacy mode: anonymous, confidential, or open identity.
6. Upload attachments if needed. Supported files are images and PDF files up to 1 MB.
7. Review the report.
8. Submit.

### Track Submissions

Open **Submissions** to search and filter your submitted reports. You can filter by category and type.

### Notifications

Open **Notifications** to see updates from administrators. Use **Mark all as read** when you have reviewed them.

## Administrator Guide

Administrators can manage the full quality assurance dataset, depending on the pages enabled in the frontend.

Common work:

- Manage faculties and departments.
- Manage courses and student records.
- Review student feedback and complaints.
- Update complaint status and add administrative comments.
- Review dashboards and analytics.
- Manage institutional documents.
- Manage accreditation records.
- Manage QA committee records.

When an administrator updates a student complaint, the student receives a notification.

## Focal Person Guide

Focal persons support departmental or faculty quality assurance workflows.

Common work:

- Review dashboard summaries.
- Track student feedback.
- Participate in QA committee workflows if assigned.
- Upload or review accreditation evidence where permitted.
- Monitor corrective actions.

## Principal Officer Guide

Principal officers use the platform to monitor quality assurance status across the institution.

Common work:

- View executive dashboard summaries.
- Review accreditation readiness.
- Monitor early warning alerts.
- Track QA committee effectiveness.
- Review student experience indicators.
- Review institutional document status.
- Generate or review reports.

## QA Committee Users

Committee users manage committee governance and improvement workflows.

Common work:

- View committees.
- Schedule meetings.
- Mark meetings as held.
- Record attendance.
- Create audit cycles.
- Submit findings.
- Add recommendations.
- Create action plans.
- Submit evidence.
- Submit and approve committee reports if permitted.

Some users can view records but cannot create or approve them. If an action is unavailable, your role may not have permission.

## Institutional Documents

Authorized users can:

1. Create document categories.
2. Upload PDF documents or add document links.
3. Submit documents for review.
4. Approve or reject documents if permitted.
5. Publish approved documents.
6. Upload new versions.
7. Preview or download visible documents.

Documents may be public, authenticated-only, restricted to QA focal persons, or private.

## Accreditation

Authorized users can:

- Create accreditation cycles.
- Manage accreditation components and metrics.
- Submit metric values.
- Upload evidence.
- Verify or reject evidence.
- Calculate component scores.
- Calculate PARI.
- Review alerts.
- Track corrective actions.

## Reports and Analytics

Dashboards and analytics show live values from platform records. Empty dashboards usually mean the relevant records have not been created yet.

## Troubleshooting

- You are redirected to sign in: your session has expired. Sign in again.
- You see unauthorized: your account is signed in but does not have permission for that page.
- A form will not submit: check required fields and validation messages.
- Upload failed: check file type and size.
- A dashboard is empty: there may be no records for your faculty, department, or time period.
- A document is missing: it may be private, archived, unpublished, or outside your role visibility.
